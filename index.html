FACTS ABOUT CRICKET

1. Cricket actually began in 16th century England when children used to play it on the streets.
2. William Gilbert (W.G.) Grace is recognized as the father of cricket.
3. It is the second most popular sport after soccer/football with an estimated fan base of 2.5 billion people.
4. Cricket is an umbrella term that includes three major types T20, One Day, and Test Match.
5. Organized by the International Cricket Council, the first Cricket World Cup happened in the year 1975.
6. The three founding countries of the World Cup are Australia, England, and South Africa.
7. Originally, cricket wickets had two stumps and only one bail. The third one was introduced in 1775.
8. A cricket bat is made from white willow wood, found in the northern hemisphere during the winter season.
9. The longest cricket match was played in Durban between England and South Africa. It lasted for 9 days with over 683 overs.
10. The shortest cricket match was played in 2009 between West Indies and England. It only lasted for 10 balls.
11. The former Indian coach Ravi Shastri is the first cricketer to hit 6 sixes in an over.
12. Former Pakistani right-arm fast bowler Shoaib Akhtar is known for his fastest delivery in the history of the cricket world. He is also known as Rawalpindi Express.
13. Iftikhar Ali Khan is the only Test cricketer to have played for both England and India.
14. Sachin Tendulkar, the God of Cricket made his debut at the early age of 16 years old.
15. Sachin Tendulkar is the first batter in the history of cricket to be given out by the third umpire.
16.Women's cricket worldcup started 2 years back when men's cricket worldcup started.

Cricket is a bat-and-ball game played between two teams of eleven players on a field, at the centre of which is a 22-yard (20-metre; 66-foot) pitch with a wicket at each end, each comprising two bails (small sticks) balanced on three stumps. Two players from the batting team, the striker and nonstriker, stand in front of either wicket holding bats, while one player from the fielding team, the bowler, bowls the ball toward the striker's wicket from the opposite end of the pitch. The striker's goal is to hit the bowled ball with the bat and then switch places with the nonstriker, with the batting team scoring one run for each of these exchanges. Runs are also scored when the ball reaches the boundary of the field or when the ball is bowled illegally.
